Behavior Guide

Social Behavior

Cockatiels are notably social birds. In the wild, they thrive in flocks and have a strong need for social interaction. As pets, they often form close bonds with their human caregivers, seeking their company and affection.

Vocalizations

Cockatiels are known for their range of vocalizations. They can whistle, chirp, and even mimic certain sounds. Males are more likely to sing melodies, while females tend to be noisier with persistent calls.

Crest Behavior

The crest of a Cockatiel is a great indicator of its mood. A raised crest might indicate curiosity or excitement, while a flattened one can show annoyance or fear. Observing the crest position can provide insights into how the bird is feeling.

Biting and Aggression

Cockatiels might bite if they are scared, provoked, or territorial. Recognizing the signs of irritation can help prevent biting. Often, a hissing sound or ruffled feathers can be a warning before a bite.

Mating Behavior

During the breeding season, Cockatiels can display mating behaviors like head bobbing, wing flapping, and tail fanning. They may also become more vocal or even territorial during this period.

Playfulness

Cockatiels are playful birds that appreciate a range of toys in their environment. They can entertain themselves for hours with bells, mirrors, and chewable toys. Interactive play with their caregivers can also be a source of joy for them.
