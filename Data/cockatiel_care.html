Care Guide

Housing

Cockatiels require a spacious cage, preferably rectangular in shape. Ensure that the cage bars are spaced no wider than 3/4 inch apart to prevent escape. Provide horizontal bars for climbing and various perches of different materials and thicknesses to promote foot health.

Diet

A balanced diet is essential:

  • Seeds: While Cockatiels naturally eat seeds, ensure they don't become the sole component of their diet.
  • Pellets: High-quality pellets can form a base of a Cockatiel's diet.
  • Fruits and Vegetables: Fresh produce provides essential vitamins and minerals. Ensure they're clean and free from pesticides.
  • Water: Always have fresh water available. Change it daily or if it becomes dirty.

Hygiene

Cleanliness is crucial:

  • Clean the cage at least once a week.
  • Provide a shallow dish for your Cockatiel to bathe in, or mist them with clean water.
  • Regularly check and trim their nails and beak if needed.

Enrichment

To prevent boredom and promote mental health:

  • Offer a variety of toys and change them frequently.
  • Introduce puzzle toys that stimulate their intellect.
  • Play music or talk to your Cockatiel; they might even mimic you!
  • Allow out-of-cage time for flight and exploration in a safe environment.

Health

Regular check-ups with an avian veterinarian are important. Watch for signs of illness like changes in behavior, appearance, or droppings. Early detection can be key to successful treatment.

Social Interaction

Cockatiels are sociable birds and thrive on interaction with their owners. Spend quality time with your bird daily. If you have a single Cockatiel, consider getting a mirror toy as a form of companionship.
